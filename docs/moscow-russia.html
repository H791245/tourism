The best time to visit Moscow is during the late spring (May) and early autumn (September) when the weather is pleasant, temperatures are mild, and the crowds are generally smaller than in summer. Summer (June-August) offers long daylight hours, warm weather, and a vibrant atmosphere with many outdoor events, but it's also peak tourist season. Winter (December-February) transforms Moscow into a magical, snow-covered wonderland, perfect for those who enjoy festive decorations and ice skating, but be prepared for extremely cold temperatures.

History

Moscow's history is a tapestry woven with power, resilience, and transformation, stretching back over eight centuries. First chronicled in 1147, it began as a minor trading post before rapidly growing into the capital of a principality, benefiting from its strategic location. By the 15th century, under rulers like Ivan the Great, Moscow had become the center of the Grand Duchy and the nucleus of a unified Russian state, establishing the Muscovite Kremlin as its fortified heart and symbol of power. It endured numerous invasions, including those by the Tatars and Poles, often rising from ashes to assert its dominance. In the early 18th century, Peter the Great shifted the capital to St. Petersburg, a move that temporarily dimmed Moscow's political star but allowed its merchant class and unique cultural identity to flourish. Napoleon's disastrous invasion in 1812 saw much of the city burned, yet Moscow rebuilt, retaining its historical significance and spiritual authority. The 20th century brought seismic change. Following the 1917 Bolshevik Revolution, Moscow reclaimed its status as the capital of Soviet Russia, and subsequently the Soviet Union. It became the nerve center of a global superpower, witnessing rapid industrialization, the horrors of Stalinist purges, and the heroic defense against Nazi Germany in WWII. The Cold War cemented its image as a formidable political stronghold. With the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991, Moscow entered a new era, transitioning to a market economy and becoming a bustling, modern metropolis, continually evolving while fiercely preserving its rich, complex heritage.
