History

Best, a municipality in the province of North Brabant, boasts a history stretching back to Roman times, with evidence of early settlements and agricultural activity by Germanic tribes like the Batavians. The name 'Best' itself is thought to derive from 'Besten,' possibly referring to a 'best crossing' or 'passage' over local waterways, highlighting its historical significance as a transit point. Originally, Best was not an independent entity but part of the larger heerlijkheid (lordship) and later municipality of Oirschot. Its strategic location, at a crossing of ancient roads and waterways, contributed to its gradual development, primarily remaining an agricultural community through the Middle Ages. A pivotal moment occurred in 1819 when Best officially separated from Oirschot, establishing itself as an independent municipality. This separation marked the beginning of its distinct identity and accelerated growth. The 19th and early 20th centuries brought further development, largely influenced by the construction of the railway line between Eindhoven and Tilburg, which passed through Best and spurred economic activity and connectivity. During World War II, Best was not spared from conflict. It experienced the impact of the war, particularly during Operation Market Garden in September 1944, when Allied forces advanced through the region, leading to intense fighting and eventual liberation. The post-war era saw rapid industrialization and significant population growth. Companies like Philips once had a substantial presence, diversifying Best's economy beyond its agricultural roots into manufacturing and technology. Today, Best is a modern, thriving community known for its excellent infrastructure, including its direct access to the A2 and A58 highways, green surroundings with several nature reserves, and a strong focus on logistics, technology, and recreation, blending its historical heritage with contemporary innovation and a high quality of life.
