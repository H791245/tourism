Story of this place

The history of public libraries in Groningen, much like in many Dutch cities, dates back to initiatives aimed at public education and access to knowledge. While not as ancient as the city's prestigious university library (founded with the University of Groningen in 1614), the public library system has evolved significantly over centuries to meet the changing needs of its citizens. For many decades, the primary public library was a beloved institution located at Oude Boteringestraat, serving generations of readers and learners with its extensive collections and community programs. However, as the 21st century progressed, there was a growing vision for a more integrated, dynamic cultural hub that could serve as a vibrant focal point for the city, embracing modern concepts of information, entertainment, and community engagement. This ambitious vision culminated in the monumental Forum Groningen project. After years of meticulous planning, complex construction, and significant investment, the Forum officially opened its striking doors in November 2019. This ultra-modern, architecturally distinctive building is far more than just a library; it is a sprawling cultural complex designed to be a "vertical city" of ideas, learning, and experiences. The public library is the vibrant heart of the Forum, occupying several beautifully designed floors with vast collections, diverse reading spaces, study areas, and cutting-edge digital resources. The Forum's development marked a significant shift in how Groningen approached public access to culture and information. It brought together various institutions under one magnificent roof, including the public library, a multi-screen cinema, Storyworld (a museum dedicated to comics, animation, and games), a media lab, workshops, event spaces, restaurants, and a stunning rooftop terrace offering unparalleled panoramic views of the city. This transformation from a traditional library building to a multifaceted cultural powerhouse reflects a modern understanding of a library's indispensable role in community engagement, innovation, and lifelong learning, solidifying its status as an indispensable destination for both residents and visitors alike.
