anne frank house, amsterdam

The Anne Frank House is centrally located in Amsterdam's historic Jordaan district, directly on the Prinsengracht canal. It's easily accessible on foot from many city center attractions, including Dam Square and the Royal Palace, and well-connected by Amsterdam's tram and bus network. The famous Westerkerk (Wester Church), whose bells Anne Frank wrote about hearing from her hiding place, is just around the corner. Story of this place

The Anne Frank House is a museum in Amsterdam dedicated to the Jewish diarist Anne Frank, who hid with her family and four other people in the annex of the building during World War II to escape Nazi persecution. From July 1942 to August 1944, Anne, her sister Margot, parents Otto and Edith Frank, Hermann and Auguste van Pels, their son Peter, and Fritz Pfeffer lived in the "Secret Annex" (Achterhuis), a concealed apartment behind a revolving bookcase on the Prinsengracht canal. The annex was located above the Opekta Works, Otto Frank's business. During this period of confinement, Anne meticulously documented her thoughts, feelings, and experiences in her diary, which she received for her 13th birthday. Their hiding place was supported by several courageous helpers, including Miep Gies and Victor Kugler, who provided food, news, and managed the business. On August 4, 1944, the group was betrayed and arrested by the Gestapo. They were transported to concentration camps. Anne and Margot both died of typhus in Bergen-Belsen in February or March 1945, just weeks before the camp's liberation. Otto Frank was the only one of the eight to survive the Holocaust. Miep Gies saved Anne's diary and gave it to Otto after the war, leading to its publication in 1947 as "Het Achterhuis" (The Secret Annex), and later internationally as "The Diary of a Young Girl." The house was preserved and opened as a museum in 1960, dedicated to Anne's story and a poignant reminder of the dangers of discrimination and persecution. It draws millions of visitors globally, offering a firsthand look into their confined world and amplifying Anne's enduring message of hope and resilience amidst profound darkness.
