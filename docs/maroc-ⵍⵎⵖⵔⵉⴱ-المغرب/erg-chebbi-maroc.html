Story of this place

Erg Chebbi's history is deeply intertwined with the vast Sahara Desert and the ancient cultures that have traversed its sands for millennia. Geologically, these majestic dunes are a product of millions of years of wind erosion, shaping sand into towering formations. Before human history, the region was part of a ancient sea, evidenced by the abundant marine fossils found in the surrounding rocky areas, particularly near Erfoud. Human presence in the region dates back to prehistoric times, with evidence of early settlements and rock art. For centuries, Erg Chebbi and its vicinity served as a critical corridor for nomadic Amazigh (Berber) tribes, who mastered life in the harsh desert environment, following ancient routes and seasonal pastures. While not a trade city itself, its proximity to historic caravan routes, particularly those leading to the legendary Sijilmassa (near modern-day Rissani), linked it indirectly to the trans-Saharan trade network that moved gold, salt, spices, and slaves between sub-Saharan Africa and the Mediterranean world. In more recent history, particularly over the last few decades, Erg Chebbi has transformed into a major tourist destination. This shift has brought new economic opportunities to local Amazigh communities, who have adapted by offering desert experiences while striving to preserve their rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life amidst the influx of visitors.
