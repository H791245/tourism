Ouarzazate Solar Power Station

The Ouarzazate Solar Power Station is located near the city of Ouarzazate in southeastern Morocco, at the edge of the vast Sahara Desert. Ouarzazate city, often dubbed 'the door of the desert' and 'Morocco's Hollywood,' is famous for its film studios (Atlas Studios, CLA Studios) and its role as a filming location for major international productions. The region offers a rich array of attractions beyond the power station, including the UNESCO World Heritage site of Ait Benhaddou (an ancient fortified village), the historic Taourirt Kasbah within Ouarzazate city, and picturesque desert landscapes perfect for excursions. The power station is accessible by road from Marrakech via the scenic Tizi n'Tichka mountain pass or by flights to Ouarzazate Airport.

Story of this place

Morocco embarked on an ambitious journey to harness its vast solar potential with the conception of the Ouarzazate Solar Power Station, a cornerstone of its national energy strategy. Driven by King Mohammed VI's vision to reduce fossil fuel dependency and become a global leader in green energy, the project was spearheaded by the Moroccan Agency for Sustainable Energy (MASEN). The complex, officially known as Noor Ouarzazate, began its development with Noor I, commissioned in 2016. This initial phase utilized 160 MW of Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) parabolic trough technology, notably equipped with 3 hours of thermal storage allowing electricity generation even after sunset. Following its success, the complex rapidly expanded with subsequent phases. Noor II, operational in 2018, added another 200 MW of CSP parabolic trough, enhancing storage capabilities to 6-8 hours. A significant technological leap was Noor III, also launched in 2018, which introduced a 150 MW CSP solar tower, standing over 240 meters tall and employing molten salt for advanced thermal storage, providing 7.5 hours of storage. Concurrently, Noor IV diversified the complex with 72 MW of Photovoltaic (PV) panels, showcasing a hybrid approach to solar energy generation. This multi-phase development, supported by substantial international funding from institutions like the World Bank, African Development Bank, and KfW, solidified the Noor Ouarzazate complex as one of the largest CSP facilities globally, covering an immense area of over 3,000 hectares. Beyond its impressive technical achievements, the power station represents a profound commitment to sustainable development, significantly reducing Morocco's carbon footprint by millions of tons annually, creating thousands of employment opportunities, and bolstering the local economy, while setting a global benchmark for large-scale renewable energy projects and demonstrating Morocco's dedication to combating climate change.
