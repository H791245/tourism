Story of this place

The Hassan Tower, or Tour Hassan, stands as a monumental testament to the ambition of the Almohad Caliph Yacoub al-Mansour. Its construction began in 1195 AD, intended to be the minaret of what would have been the world's largest mosque. This grand mosque was envisioned to accommodate his victorious army returning from their campaigns in Spain. Al-Mansour's vision, however, was abruptly cut short with his death in 1199. Consequently, both the mosque and its colossal minaret were left unfinished. The tower reached only 44 meters (144 feet) ‚Äì less than half of its projected 86-meter (282 feet) height. The remains of the adjacent mosque, today visible as hundreds of columns, hint at its enormous original footprint. The site suffered further devastation in 1755 when the powerful Lisbon earthquake largely destroyed the incomplete mosque structure, leaving only the sturdy tower and the remnants of its foundational pillars. Today, the Hassan Tower stands as an iconic symbol of Rabat, representing a significant piece of Moroccan and Almohad history. Its architectural style, characterized by intricate geometric patterns, influenced other famous Almohad minarets like the Giralda in Seville and the Kutubiyya minaret in Marrakech. Adjacent to the tower is the magnificent Mausoleum of Mohammed V, making the entire complex a vital national and historical site, recognized for its cultural significance.
