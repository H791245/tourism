Summer months (June-August) can be famously foggy and chilly, often obscuring the bridge from view.

Golden Gate Bridge, US

The Golden Gate Bridge dramatically spans the Golden Gate Strait, the narrow channel where San Francisco Bay meets the Pacific Ocean. It serves as a vital artery connecting the northern tip of the San Francisco Peninsula (the city of San Francisco) to Marin County on the north side. The bridge is part of U.S. Route 101 and California State Route 1. Key vantage points for viewing include the Presidio and Fort Point on the San Francisco side, and the Marin Headlands (such as Battery Spencer and Vista Point) on the Marin side.

Story of this place

The dream of bridging the treacherous Golden Gate Strait, connecting San Francisco to Marin County, emerged in the early 20th century. For decades, the strait's formidable challenges‚Äîstrong currents, deep water, persistent fog, powerful winds, and seismic activity‚Äîled many to deem the project 'impossible.' Chief Engineer Joseph Strauss, despite initial skepticism and numerous design iterations, relentlessly championed the idea. He was supported by a brilliant team, including Leon S. Moisseiff for the suspension design, Charles Alton Ellis for the intricate calculations, and Irving F. Morrow, who not only refined the Art Deco architectural elements but also selected the iconic 'International Orange' color for its visibility in fog and harmony with the natural surroundings. Construction began on January 5, 1933, amidst the Great Depression, providing much-needed employment. The project was monumental, employing innovative engineering techniques and prioritizing worker safety‚Äîa revolutionary concept for its time. A crucial safety net stretched beneath the bridge deck saved 19 lives, earning those workers the moniker 'Halfway-to-Hell Club.' Despite these measures, 11 men tragically lost their lives during the arduous four-and-a-half-year construction. Completed ahead of schedule and under budget at an approximate cost of $35 million, the Golden Gate Bridge officially opened to pedestrians on May 27, 1937, followed by vehicular traffic the next day. It was immediately hailed as an engineering marvel and a symbol of American ingenuity and perseverance. Beyond its practical function, the bridge swiftly became an enduring global icon, renowned for its stunning aesthetics and breathtaking setting.
