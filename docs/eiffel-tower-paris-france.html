The best time to visit the Eiffel Tower is during the shoulder seasons: Spring (April-May) and Autumn (September-October). During these months, the weather is pleasant with comfortable temperatures, and the crowds are generally smaller than the peak summer months. Visiting during late afternoon or early evening allows you to experience both daylight views and the tower sparkling at night.

History

The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris and France, was conceived as the grand entrance to the 1889 World's Fair, celebrating the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Designed by Gustave Eiffel's company, with engineers Maurice Koechlin and Ã‰mile Nouguier and architect Stephen Sauvestre, its construction began in January 1887. For two years, two months, and five days, 300 workers meticulously assembled 18,038 pieces of wrought iron using 2.5 million rivets. Standing at 324 meters (1,063 feet) tall, it was, upon completion, the tallest man-made structure in the world, a title it held for 41 years. Initially, the tower faced fierce opposition from prominent artists and intellectuals who decried its 'monstrous' appearance, fearing it would permanently mar the Parisian skyline. Many signed a petition against its construction, viewing it as a temporary steel 'colossus' that would be an eyesore. Despite the controversy, it was a resounding success during the Exposition Universelle, attracting millions of visitors. Gustave Eiffel had secured a concession to keep the tower standing for 20 years, after which it was slated for demolition. However, its strategic value as a platform for radio telegraphy, particularly for military communications during World War I, saved it from being dismantled. Over the decades, the Eiffel Tower transformed from a controversial temporary exhibit into an enduring symbol of French engineering prowess, culture, and romance, becoming one of the most recognized and visited monuments globally.
